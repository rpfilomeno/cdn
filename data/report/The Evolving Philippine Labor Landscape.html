Fastest Declining Job Sectors (Non-AI Driven)

Recent data indicates significant job losses in several sectors, primarily driven by non-technological factors like weather conditions, seasonal cycles, and policy changes.

Year-on-Year Declines (Mar 2024 - Mar 2025)

Job losses in thousands

The Agriculture and Forestry sector experienced the largest drop, losing 609,000 jobs, largely due to adverse weather. Public Administration and Defense also saw a significant reduction.

Month-on-Month Declines (Feb 2025 - Mar 2025)

Job losses in thousands

Construction faced the sharpest recent drop (-473,000 jobs), followed by Wholesale and Retail Trade. These highlight immediate pressures on employment likely due to project cycles and specific economic activities.

The Rise of AI: Exposure & Broad Impact

Artificial Intelligence is not yet the primary driver of the largest numerical job declines but is a critical transformative force reshaping roles across industries.

AI Displacement Risk

Portion of total workforce (IMF Projection)

The IMF projects that 14% of the Philippine workforce faces a direct risk of displacement by AI.

AI Job Exposure & Complementarity

Nature of AI's impact on tasks

While 35-40% of jobs are "highly exposed" to AI, a significant portion (60-61% of those) are "highly complementary," suggesting AI will augment rather than replace, provided upskilling occurs.

Charting a Future-Ready Philippine Labor Market

The Philippine labor market is at a pivotal juncture. While traditional sectors face non-AI driven declines, AI is reshaping the nature of work. The path forward lies not in resisting AI, but in strategically adapting to it. By investing in human capital, fostering digital readiness, prioritizing upskilling, and promoting human-AI collaboration, the Philippines can navigate this transformation, turning challenges into opportunities for a more productive, resilient, and inclusive economic future. The focus must be on future-proofing the workforce to harness AI's potential for national development and individual prosperity.
